Synopsis

Five Feet Apart tells the emotional story of Stella, a determined and organized teen who has lived most of her life inside hospital walls due to cystic fibrosis. She follows every rule and procedure to stay healthy, finding comfort in routines and control. Her world shifts when she meets Will, a sarcastic and charming boy with the same illness, but with a more dangerous strain that puts other patients at risk.

Despite their opposite personalities, Stella and Will form a bond built on shared struggles, late-night conversations, and quiet understanding. Their feelings slowly deepen, but the strict rule of staying physically apart becomes the biggest barrier between them. They find creative ways to connect without touching, balancing their desire for closeness with the life-threatening consequences of breaking the distance.

As their relationship grows, Stella must decide how much she is willing to risk in order to truly live, while Will must learn that love sometimes means stepping back. Their journey becomes a heartfelt reminder that even when touch is impossible, hope, love, and connection can survive—sometimes at exactly five feet apart.
