Synopsis

To All the Boys I’ve Loved Before tells the story of Lara Jean Covey, a shy high-school junior whose love life has only existed in her imagination. For every boy she has ever had strong feelings for, Lara Jean writes a heartfelt letter—then hides it away, never intending for anyone to read them.

Her peaceful, invisible life takes a sudden turn when all five letters are mysteriously mailed out. Embarrassed and overwhelmed, Lara Jean panics, especially when one of the letters reaches Peter Kavinsky, a charming and well-known student at her school. To save face and avoid awkwardness with her sister’s ex-boyfriend (who also gets a letter), Lara Jean agrees to fake-date Peter.

As they spend more time together, their pretend relationship begins to feel real. Lara Jean must confront her insecurities, her fears of opening up, and the possibility that love might be more complicated—and more beautiful—than she imagined.
